Crypto Mining Tax Strategy

Turn mining hardware into first-year depreciation deductions and ongoing cash flow, fully structured for IRS compliance.

How Crypto Mining Creates Tax Deductions and Cash Flow

Cryptocurrency mining hardware is classified as tangible personal property under the Internal Revenue Code and depreciates as 5-year MACRS property (IRC Sec. 168(a)). When structured properly, a crypto mining operation can generate significant first-year depreciation deductions while also producing ongoing cash flow from mined cryptocurrency.

A typical $100,000 investment in mining hardware can generate approximately 70% first-year depreciation (combining bonus depreciation and MACRS accelerated methods), resulting in roughly $70,000 in deductions in Year 1. The mining operation also produces approximately $20,000 per year in cash flow (roughly 0.25 BTC at current market rates), creating both a tax benefit and an income-producing asset.

Who This Strategy Serves

This strategy is designed for business owners and high-income W-2 earners who want to create legitimate depreciation deductions while building a cash-flowing asset. The mining operation must be conducted as a trade or business (not a hobby) to claim depreciation deductions. Proper entity structuring, documentation, and profit motive are essential for compliance.

Tax Treatment

Mining hardware depreciates under the Modified Accelerated Cost Recovery System (MACRS) with a 5-year recovery period. Bonus depreciation under IRC Sec. 168(k) allows a significant percentage of the cost to be deducted in the year the equipment is placed in service.

Income from mined cryptocurrency is treated as ordinary income at the fair market value on the date it is received (IRS Notice 2014-21). This income is subject to self-employment tax if the mining is conducted as a sole proprietorship. Proper entity structuring can help manage the self-employment tax exposure.

Ongoing Cash Flow

Beyond the depreciation benefit, the mining hardware continues to produce cryptocurrency. The mined coins can be held (with potential capital appreciation), sold for cash, or reinvested. The ongoing cash flow helps offset the initial investment and creates a real, income-producing business.

Compliance Requirements

The IRS requires that mining activities be conducted with a genuine profit motive and operated as a legitimate business. Proper records must be maintained for equipment costs, electricity expenses, mining output, and fair market values at the time of receipt.
