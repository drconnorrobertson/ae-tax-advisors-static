Equipment Leasing / Section 179 Tax Strategy

Put 10% down, 3% closing costs, and write off 100% of the equipment cost up to $512K in a single tax year.

How Equipment Leasing Creates Immediate Tax Deductions

Section 179 of the Internal Revenue Code allows business owners to deduct the full purchase price of qualifying equipment in the year it is placed in service, rather than depreciating it over several years. For 2026, the deduction limit is $512,000 (adjusted annually for inflation under IRC Sec. 179(b)).

Equipment leasing structures allow you to put as little as 10% down with approximately 3% in closing costs, while still claiming the full Sec. 179 deduction on the total equipment cost. This means you can generate a $512,000 deduction with a fraction of that amount in out-of-pocket capital.

Who Qualifies

To claim a Sec. 179 deduction, you must have an active trade or business. This applies to business owners, self-employed individuals, and W-2 earners who also operate a qualifying side business. The equipment must be used more than 50% for business purposes and must be placed in service during the tax year in which the deduction is claimed.

Qualifying equipment includes machinery, vehicles over 6,000 lbs GVWR, office equipment, technology hardware, and certain software. The property must be tangible personal property used in the active conduct of a trade or business (IRC Sec. 179(d)(1)).

The Tax Impact

For a business owner or W-2 earner in the 37% federal bracket, a $512,000 Sec. 179 deduction can reduce federal income tax liability by up to approximately $189,000 in a single year. When combined with state income tax savings, the total tax reduction can be even more substantial.

The equipment also generates income or utility for your business, making this a strategy that produces both tax savings and operational value.

Important Compliance Notes

The Sec. 179 deduction cannot exceed your taxable business income for the year (IRC Sec. 179(b)(3)(A)). Any excess can be carried forward to future years. The total cost of equipment placed in service during the year must not exceed $2,890,000 (2026 phase-out threshold), after which the deduction begins to phase out dollar for dollar.

This is not a loophole. Section 179 is an IRS-sanctioned provision designed to incentivize business investment. Proper documentation, business-use substantiation, and correct filing are required.
